Step 1: Identify Automation Opportunities

Before diving into tools, you need to understand what to automate. The best candidates are tasks that are:

  • Repetitive: You do the same thing multiple times per week or month
  • Rule-based: There's a clear process or decision tree
  • Time-consuming: The task takes significant time to complete manually
  • Low-value: It doesn't require your unique expertise or creativity

Step 2: Map Your Current Workflow

Once you've identified candidates, document your current process. This helps you identify where automation fits best.

1 Document Each Step

Write down every action you take, in order. Be specific.

Example: New Lead Follow-up

  1. Lead fills out contact form on website
  2. I receive notification email
  3. I manually add lead to CRM
  4. I copy-paste personalized template into email
  5. I adjust the email based on their inquiry
  6. I send the email
  7. I create a follow-up task for 3 days later

2 Identify Bottlenecks

Look for steps that:

  • Take the most time
  • Are most prone to error
  • Could benefit from AI assistance (like writing personalized emails)
  • Require constant manual attention

3 Design Your Automated Flow

Map what the ideal automated version looks like:

Automated Version:

  1. Lead fills out form â†’ automatically added to CRM
  2. AI generates personalized response based on their inquiry
  3. Response sent immediately
  4. Lead added to email sequence
  5. Follow-up task created automatically

Step 4: Build Your First Automation

Start simple. Your first automation should be straightforward and high-impact.

1 Choose a Simple Starting Point

Recommended first automations:

  • Send a welcome email when someone signs up for your newsletter
  • Add new form submissions to a spreadsheet
  • Post new blog content to social media
  • Send a reminder when a task is overdue
  • Save email attachments to cloud storage automatically

2 Use a Template

Both Zapier and Make have thousands of pre-built templates. Search for your use case and customize it rather than building from scratch.

3 Test Thoroughly

Before relying on an automation:

  • Run multiple test scenarios
  • Check edge cases (what happens if data is missing?)
  • Verify all outputs are correct
  • Monitor for the first few days
